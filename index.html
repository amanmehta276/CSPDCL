Substation Summary

The Nawapara Rajim 33/11 kV Substation is operated by Chhattisgarh State Power Distribution Company Limited (CSPDCL) under the Rajim Division, Raipur Circle. Commissioned in 2008, it plays a vital role in distributing power to Nawapara Rajim and surrounding rural and urban areas.


The substation is equipped with two 5 MVA power transformers operating at 33/11 kV, with two incoming feeders at 33 kV (one continuous incomer and one feedback incomer), and twelve outgoing feeders — four at 33 kV and eight at 11 kV — serving residential, agricultural, commercial, and industrial consumers.

About the Developer
Aman Mehta
EEE Student, Government Engineering College/ Chhattisgarh Institute of Technology, Raipur

This portal is designed and built by Vyom, a full-stack developer with a strong interest in taking real engineering data out of paper registers and making it interactive. Alongside his EEE coursework, he runs VyomsTech, building web apps, dashboards, and tools for local businesses and technical projects.


This substation portal grew out of his 6th-semester vocational training at CSPDCL's Nawapara Rajim 33/11 kV Substation. Instead of leaving the training report as a static document, he modeled the substation's actual single line diagram, feeder network, transformers, and protection equipment into a live, clickable dashboard — so anyone can explore how power actually flows through the 33/11 kV system, feeder by feeder.
